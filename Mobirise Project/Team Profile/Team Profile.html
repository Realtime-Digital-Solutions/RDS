Collective Team Myers Briggs' Personality Results

Team Profile

According to our Myers Briggs' test results, the group collectively falls under the INTJ-A Architect Analyst personality type. We are good at ignoring distractions when focused on work and like to work efficiently. We tend to focus on functional team building techniques rather than social activities. Despite our introverted nature, we are known to have irreverent wit and share a sarcastic sense of humour under our more serious barriers. To those who manage to break the ice with the team, beware!
In our work, we endeavour to find efficient ways to complete tasks and are not afraid to think outside of the box, but we will consider more realistic and workable solutions. Our team also comprises of members who have contrasting personality traits to help balance our collective personality.

Differences Comparison:

Declan is the extrovert in the team, which will help us to engage with our environment and push limits.
Declan and Matthew bring observant traits to the team, helping to keep the team tethered to our goals.
Matthew and Zachary bring feeling traits to the team, helping us to identify the "whos" rather than the "whats".
Declan also counters the teams judging nature by being prospective, with his flexibility assisting the team when unexpected challenges arise.
Zach and Shane counter the teams' assertive nature with their turbulent personality traits. They bring perfectionist ideals to the team and a drive continually improve on our work.
Rachel's personality profile matches the teams personality profile with no outlying traits. But she does show close balances between her thinking/feeling and assertive/turbulent traits. Cameron, Declan and Matthew also have some close balancing traits. Having these balances in the teams personality profile will help provide flexibility and ubiquity amongst the team.

RDS Team Learning Styles Pie Chart

Team Learning Style

Cameron, Declan and Shane have a dominant kinesthetic learning style. They learn by doing and reflecting on previous experiences. Matthew and Zachary are inclined to visual learning styles. They find it easier to learn through visual cues and reading. Rachel favours auditory learning styles. Finding it easier to absorb information through verbal reports and listening.
We have a cascading mix of learning styles within the team. Cameron, Declan and Shane can help support the team by sharing knowledge through their experiences. Matthew, Zachary and Rachel can help support Cameron, Declan and Shane with visual and auditory information. Rachel is the only individual suited to the auditory learning style, which may be challenging for her to support the entire team when working with this kind of information. But due to the diversity of information and how it can be accessed and presented in many different forms, this should not be much of an issue.
