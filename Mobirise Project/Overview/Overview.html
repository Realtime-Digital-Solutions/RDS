Traditional Holter Monitoring

(Figure 1)

Sourced: Directly from Website. Wearable Heart Rhythm Monitors: Which One, When, and Why?

Once a physician is alerted to a potential cardiac rhythm issue, they often prefer to monitor with an approved medical device such as a traditional Holter monitoring device. These battery-operated devices are about the size of a postcard. The patient usually wears it via a strap around the neck or an elastic band around the waist. There are EKG (Electrocardiogram) leads that attach to the chest.

The device collects data recorded from the ECG continuously for a given time. Typically, it is used anywhere from 24 - 48 hours to a week or more. The challenge with Holter monitors is that they can be cumbersome and delay important treatment. Patients must return the device to the doctor’s office. Then, the doctor must download and analyse the data before meeting again with the patient to discuss the results. If the problem is not detected during this monitoring period, the cardiologist may prescribe additional time wearing the Holter. Or he may prescribe an implantable continuous remote monitoring device instead.

Fitness Heart Rate Monitors

The traditional Holter monitor, even though effective and brilliant for its time, is bulky and cumbersome to wear for the patient. It also requires constant monitoring from the patient to be in the correct body position as not to obstruct and hinder any sensors, wiring and physical buttons on the device. Any minor heart palpitations the patient does suffer, must be recorded with the press of a physical button or the data is not recorded. Any major cardiac issues need to be reported in the manner of a phone call to emergency services, some of which time constraints is the limiting factor in saving someone’s life. It does not have any waterproof capability, there are tremendous costs to the medical industry if the device is damaged and it must be physically returned to the appropriate sources for data analysation. All of which has huge ramifications if any unorthodox incidents were to occur in the set time frame as well as the possibility for corrupted data. The patient must also reinitialise the procedure in accordance with the Cardiologist’s recommendation if there are any unusual circumstances. For these inconveniences, we believe that this is obsolete technology.

Figure 2

Fitness Heart Rate monitors were mainly seen as a gimmick to the consumer market when it released. It is in our personal unified opinion that if a device is not introduced through the medical system, it is deemed along the lines of being a technological want, rather than a need. The health system in general, its products, devices and resources undergo rigorous testing procedures in order to pass quality control and adherence to the Australian Government Department of Health Australian regulatory guidelines for medical devices.

The main feature that we offer in our iteration on the Heart Rate Monitor is location capability of the patient. This relieves the stress on the paramedics and medical personnel by providing them with data to pinpoint the location of the patient, thus reducing the overall time spent searching. We also have the capability to send this data direct over Wi-Fi to a medical professional for analysis and decision making. As ideas are explored, more features will come online to realise its potential and mitigate any issues, leveraging existing and new technology. Our product aims to fix these issues by analysing the data that we have gathered about these products and possibly speaking to the parties involved, both in the professional manufacturing and medical industry to gain an insight into to the needs of the patient and the healthcare system.
