Risks


As a group we have considered adverse issues relating to our prototype and where appropriate steps taken to address them.

Potential Issues:

Strain on ambulance system;

Additional pressure on healthcare system;

Costs associated with around clock patient care;

Liability of technology failure;

Requires government backing;

Third party hardware not working as expected (in particular heartrate sensor);

Strain on ambulance system: Once “PulseBeat” is fully functional instead of using ambulances to transport patients, we can create an alternative transport division. Our patients will not require immediate medical care due to early detection, we believe our innovation will reduce pressure on ambulances in the future.

Additional pressure on healthcare system: The data from “PulseBeat” must be monitored by cardiologists around the clock and this will require a significant number of specialists. With improvements to technology, we expect a favourable shift in the patient to cardiologist ratio.

Costs associated with around clock patient care: As demand for “PulseBeat” grows, the healthcare industry will benefit from job creation. This will provide an opportunity to scale the business and reduce overall costs for patients.

Liability associated with technology failure: At “PulseBeat” we understand the importance of Systems Development Life Cycle and intend to allocate significant resources to testing. 

No product is infallible however, with the right people in place strategies can be implemented to minimise risk where possible.

Requires government backing: Given the immanent dangers associated to patients and to provide care in a time sensitive manner it will be important to have the backing of both private and public hospitals.

Third party hardware not working as expected: As part of our project development, we purchased a heart rate monitor sensor to test our technology. We found the heart rate sensor to be inaccurate and overly sensitive. This results in the patient's heart rate were unstable and inconsistent. This sensor will not be used commercially and only serves the purpose of basic testing.

Benefits vs. Risks
The benefits to individuals in the community far outweigh the risks associated with our prototype.
To provide funding, consideration of government grants or support programs should be considered, due to the significant benefits our device would provide to both the community and healthcare industry, as a whole.
