Scopes & Limits


We understand that software and projects are limitless. What this means is they require continued time and development. Our vision was to develop software which enables medical staff to easily identify when a patient is experiencing an irregular heart rate.

For example, our software illustrates a patient's heart rate, however this data comes through as a MAC address and not the patient's name. For a professional who is monitoring patients a MAC address means nothing and if they could select a patient by name this would be much more impactful and allow our technology to operate efficiently.

There were several other features and functions we didn’t have the time to add, some of them critical to the applications functionality and usability, even as a prototype.

These were:

Time of data arrival in application.

Multiple devices detected on the same application.
RSSI level text colour to change when device has a weak signal.

The MAC address is associated with the patient’s name.

The emergency alarm and flashing screen.

If we had more time, these functions would be part of our prototype. Development of the Arduino code took longer than expected as the library for the Maxin oximeter sensor and algorithm are less than effective. We were second guessing ourselves believing it was our code or hardware that was at fault. After searching the forums for an answer, it seems that many are experiencing the same issue and no one has a solution as yet. The Python and GUI coding were a little more straightforward.

We initially set out to produce something very basic and rough as the time limit was constraining. What we have produced is working in its most simple form and requires a great deal of refinement before a commercial release.
